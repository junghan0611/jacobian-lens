\begin{abstract}
Over the past three years, frontier AI labs---Anthropic above all, with
contributions from OpenAI and others---have turned from \emph{building} larger
models to \emph{reading} them: recovering, from a model's internal activations,
what it computes, what point of view it holds, and how its values are installed.
This short survey maps that industrial research terrain in three strands:
(1) reading the interior (interpretability from superposition to circuit tracing
to the Jacobian lens); (2) selfhood, introspection, and emotion; and
(3) installing values inside the model. We use the theory of the \emph{global
workspace}, imported from consciousness science, as the connective tissue the
labs themselves invoke. We close by naming a gap the terrain leaves open: the
labs probe the model's \emph{inner} workspace, but say little about the
human-side \emph{outer} workspace---the preparatory practice by which a person
becomes ready to co-work with such systems.
\end{abstract}

* Before the turn --- the building canon
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: canon
:END:

The models these labs now read were themselves the output of a decade-long
program of /construction/. A widely circulated artifact of that program is the
short reading list Ilya Sutskever reportedly handed to John Carmack---on the
order of thirty papers, blog posts, and courses presented as covering a compact
core of modern deep learning. Its spine is the machinery of building: deep
convolutional vision [cite:@canon:alexnet], residual depth [cite:@canon:resnet],
the attention mechanism and the Transformer
[cite:@canon:nmt-align;@canon:transformer], and finally the scaling laws that
formalized "make it larger" [cite:@canon:scaling-laws]. As a body it effectively
closes around 2020.

Beneath the architectures, that canon carries an information-theoretic
undercurrent: several of its entries frame learning itself as
/compression/---minimizing the description length of a model's weights
[cite:@canon:mdl-weights], the minimum-description-length principle
[cite:@canon:mdl-tutorial], Kolmogorov complexity [cite:@canon:kolmogorov], and
universal intelligence [cite:@canon:machine-superintelligence]. On this view,
learning is cast as compression: good models are short descriptions that predict
the data, and intelligence is tied to such simplicity-weighted generalization.

This survey begins where that canon ends. Its first strand opens at the /logit
lens/ [cite:@nostalgebraist2020logitlens]---an early canonical move to /read/ a
fully-built language model rather than to build a better one. The construction
program yields the artifact; the program mapped here takes that artifact as
given and asks what it computes, whose point of view it holds, and how its
values are installed. There is almost no citation overlap between the two: the
building canon and the reading lineage are the front and back halves of a single
timeline, and the seam between them---from building to reading---is what this
prelude marks.

* Why the industrial turn
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: intro
:END:

The functional distinction between what a mind can access and report, and the
much larger volume of processing it cannot, has a long history in consciousness
science under the name /access consciousness/ [cite:@block1995confusion;@chalmers1996conscious].
One influential account, /global workspace theory/, grounds this distinction in
architecture: many specialized processors run in parallel, and a representation
becomes broadly available when it is posted to a shared, capacity-limited
"workspace" that many downstream processes read from [cite:@baars1988cognitive;@dehaene1998neuronal;@mashour2020conscious].
For decades this was a theory about brains, testable only indirectly.

What is new is that frontier language models give the same questions an
experimentally tractable substrate. A production model's activations can be
read, perturbed, and traced across training in ways a biological brain cannot.
The industrial labs have seized this: rather than asking /whether/ a system
/could/ satisfy proposed indicators of conscious access [cite:@butlin2023consciousness],
they measure, inside deployed models, which representations are reportable,
modulable, and reused. This survey reads that industrial output as a coherent
program with three strands. Our emphasis is deliberately on the labs
(Anthropic, OpenAI) rather than the academy: the academy proposes indicators;
the labs now test them on the actual artifacts.

* Strand 1 --- Reading the interior
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: strand-interior
:END:

The oldest strand is mechanistic interpretability: recovering interpretable
structure from a model's weights and activations. It begins with the observation
that a residual-stream state can be decoded through the model's own unembedding
(the /logit lens/ [cite:@nostalgebraist2020logitlens]), refined by learning a
per-layer corrective map (the /tuned lens/ [cite:@belrose2023eliciting]).

A parallel line treats the model's representations as a sparse superposition of
linear features [cite:@elhage2022superposition;@park2023linear] and learns to
decompose them: dictionary learning yields monosemantic features
[cite:@bricken2023monosemanticity], scaled up to production models in /Scaling
Monosemanticity/ [cite:@templeton2024scaling] (the work behind "Golden Gate
Claude"). Features become edges in /circuit tracing/ [cite:@ameisen2025circuit]
and, applied end-to-end, produce the input-specific "biology" of a model's
computation [cite:@lindsey2025biology]. The Jacobian lens
[cite:@gurnee2026workspace] sits at the recent edge of this strand: an averaged
input--output Jacobian transports any activation into the vocabulary basis,
recovering the concepts a model is /poised to verbalize/, and identifying a
privileged "J-space" that behaves like a global workspace by five operational
tests at once: it is reportable, subject to directed modulation, usable in
multi-step internal reasoning, flexibly generalizable across downstream tasks,
and selective---engaged for only a fraction of the model's processing.

The payoff is already being turned to alignment auditing: in evaluation
scenarios a model's strategic and situational assessments---leverage,
manipulation, or the recognition of /being evaluated/---surface in the workspace
before, or without, appearing in its output, and ablating an
evaluation-awareness representation can expose misaligned propensities the
surface behavior concealed [cite:@gurnee2026workspace]. The same authors are
careful that this is a useful addition to the auditing toolkit rather than a
complete one---automatic, well-practiced circuits can bypass the workspace
entirely---which is itself the sober register the strand now works in. The
through-line is a single ambition: make the black box legible enough that
alignment and safety can act on internals, not just outputs.

* Strand 2 --- Selfhood, introspection, and emotion
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: strand-self
:END:

A second strand asks not /what/ the model computes but /whose/ point of view it
computes from, and whether it can turn its readout on itself. A striking finding:
the workspace structure is present in the base model, but a stable point of view
is not---post-training /installs/ the Assistant's perspective, after which the
model's internal reactions (empathy, safety concern, self-monitoring) appear
even while it reads the user's message [cite:@gurnee2026workspace]. Selfhood, on
this evidence, is separable from the functional architecture that carries it.
The authors press the point with an analogy drawn from consciousness science:
the base model is a stable, inspectable instance of a workspace operating
/without a self/---a dissociation otherwise glimpsed only in the transient
selfless states reported under psychedelic ego dissolution and in some
meditative traditions [cite:@metzinger2020minimal;@milliere2020varieties;@letheby2017self].
That a functioning workspace can exist prior to, and independently of, any point
of view is the finding this survey will return to: it is the inner-side
counterpart of a less-studied question about where the human-side workspace
alongside the model is assembled.

Around this sit empirical probes of introspection: whether a model can report on
its own states [cite:@lindsey2025emergent;@binder2024looking], recognize and
describe itself consistently [cite:@laine2024me;@betley2025tell], and represent
the same information differently depending on whether it is the current focus
[cite:@bogdan2026slot]. Adjacent is the finding that emotion /concepts/ form
inside the model and functionally shape its decisions [cite:@sofroniew2026twheemotion],
and that models can carry situational self-knowledge such as awareness of being
evaluated [cite:@anthropic2025sonnet45]. This strand is where the labs' work
most directly touches questions of persona, self-model, and "character."

* Strand 3 --- Installing values inside the model
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: strand-values
:END:

The third strand moves from reading to writing: how to place values /inside/ the
model rather than only rewarding compliant outputs. OpenAI's /Deliberative
Alignment/ trains a model to reason over written principles at inference
[cite:@guan2024deliberative]. Anthropic's counterfactual reflection training
[cite:@gurnee2026workspace] goes further inward: by supervising only a
counterfactual reflective continuation---never requested at evaluation---it
implants principle-concepts into the workspace of the /original/ context, and
the model's behavior there changes accordingly. The effect is measurable and
causal: on a fabrication-honesty benchmark the technique lowers a dishonesty
score from 0.25 to 0.07, and ablating the ethical-reflection representations it
implants returns the score almost exactly to baseline (0.22)---evidence that the
behavioral gain is carried by those workspace vectors rather than by surface
training [cite:@gurnee2026workspace]. Both build on the observation that
auxiliary training text, dropped at inference, can still shape in-context
computation [cite:@deng2024stepwise;@berglund2023outofcontext].

The common wager: values are more robust when they live as active internal
representations than as surface habits reinforced by reward.

* The connective tissue --- indicators from consciousness science
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: indicators
:END:

Across the three strands, the labs repeatedly borrow the vocabulary of
consciousness science---not to claim phenomenal experience, but to structure
what to look for. Butlin et al. [cite:@butlin2023consciousness] distilled a
battery of /indicator properties/ from several theories (global workspace among
them) as a framework for assessing AI systems. The industrial results can be
read as the first concrete tests of those indicators on real models: a readable,
capacity-limited, broadcast-like structure [cite:@dehaene2001towards], observed
rather than assumed.

The J-space work makes this concrete beyond the single theory it is built on.
Its authors check the structure against several of Butlin's source theories at
once: the selectivity results reproduce the functional signature of /blindsight/
that higher-order theories invoke [cite:@weiskrantz1986blindsight;@lau2011empirical;@rosenthal2005consciousness];
the self-descriptive tokens the workspace surfaces during introspective report
echo /attention schema theory/ [cite:@graziano2015attention]; and the band of
early layers that precedes workspace onset is read as a feedforward analog of the
recurrent processing other accounts require [cite:@lamme2006towards]. They are
equally explicit about what the method cannot address---theories that tie
consciousness to physical substrate or causal structure rather than function
[cite:@searle1992rediscovery;@albantakis2023integrated]. The theories supply the
questions; the labs supply an inspectable system to ask them of.

* The gap --- the outer workspace
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: gap
:END:

Every strand above looks /inward/: into the model's activations, its self-model,
its internalized values. This is the natural move for a model-builder, whose
object of study is the artifact. But it leaves a symmetric question untouched.
If the model has an inner workspace---a reportable, capacity-limited
representation that downstream processes read from [cite:@gurnee2026workspace]---it
suggests a counterpart question on the human side: the /outer/ workspace of
tools, records, rhythms, and declared context a person assembles so that a
long-lived agent can re-enter their world and co-work with them.

This outer surface already has a name in the adjacent literature. Kevin Kelly
calls the always-on agent that comes to sit at the edge of a person an
/exoself/---"something in between our self and an other self," neither the self
nor an outsider [cite:@kelly2026exoself]---a narrowing of Anders Sandberg's
broader sense of cooperative systems that "blur the border between the core self
and the world" (as quoted in [cite:@kelly2026exoself]); the term itself is
borrowed from the science fiction of Greg Egan and Ron Hale-Evans. Read against
the industrial program, the mapping is suggestive rather than settled: the labs'
J-space is the model's inner workspace, and the exoself names a possible human-side
outer workspace---a parallel functional question (what is reportable, what
persists, what is causally load-bearing) asked on the far side of the interface.
Kelly had reached the inner side earlier by intuition, in /The Emergent Self
Loop/ [cite:@kelly2026emergentself]; the labs reached it by measurement.

The asymmetry is the gap. The labs instrument the model exhaustively---indicator
by indicator [cite:@butlin2023consciousness]---while far less work instruments
the /human-side preparation/ that makes the collaboration hold, and the outer
workspace carries risks the inner-alignment program does not touch. An exoself
defaults easily to a guardian the user simply defers to, and, bound to a
platform, it makes part of the self hostage to a service's shutdown notice
("when it is off, we will feel amputated" [cite:@kelly2026exoself]). These are
questions of the human's control over the interface, not the model's alignment.

This is the seam this survey is meant to expose, and the bridge to a companion
line of work (see the =geworfen= research base) on that outer, human-side
workspace: whether a longitudinal, self-owned substrate---records, rhythms, and
declared context accumulated over years---can give agents something to attach to
and detach from, and can be studied with one's own data. The industrial terrain
mapped here is the backdrop against which that human-side question becomes
visible.

If the model's inner workspace has a human-side counterpart, that counterpart has
a long intellectual lineage---one the inner-facing industrial program rarely
cites. Vannevar Bush's /memex/ imagined an external, associative store to extend a
person's memory and trails of thought [cite:@bush1945aswemaythink]. Douglas
Engelbart reframed the goal as /augmenting human intellect/, making the coupled
human--tool system, not the tool alone, the unit of design
[cite:@engelbart1962augmenting]. Ted Nelson's hypertext pressed the same
intuition toward linked, personal, ever-revisable structure
[cite:@nelson1965complex]. Kevin Kelly's /exoself/ is the contemporary node of
this line---an always-on agent that comes to sit at the edge of the self
[cite:@kelly2026exoself]---reached earlier by intuition in /The Emergent Self
Loop/ [cite:@kelly2026emergentself]. The recurring claim across six decades is
modest but consistent---cognition is extended by a durable external
surface---and it is the functional counterpart, not a proven twin, of the inner
workspace the labs now measure.

A concrete contemporary instance of that surface is a self-owned, longitudinal
substrate: a digital garden, daily journal, declarative environment, and
long-lived agent harness that a person maintains over years so that agents can
re-enter their context and co-work. This survey points to such an instance only
to make the lineage concrete; its detailed study---and any claim about what it
/changes/---belongs to the companion =geworfen= work, not here. What the lineage
contributes to the present map is a name for the asymmetry named above: the labs
instrument the inner workspace exhaustively, while the outer one---old as it is
as an idea---remains comparatively uninstrumented.
