\begin{abstract}
Over the past three years, frontier AI labs---Anthropic above all, with
contributions from OpenAI and others---have turned from \emph{building} larger
models to \emph{reading} them: recovering, from a model's internal activations,
what it computes, what point of view it holds, and how its values are installed.
This short survey maps that industrial research terrain in three strands:
(1) reading the interior (interpretability from superposition to circuit tracing
to the Jacobian lens); (2) selfhood, introspection, and emotion; and
(3) installing values inside the model. We use the theory of the \emph{global
workspace}, imported from consciousness science, as the connective tissue the
labs themselves invoke. We close by naming a gap the terrain leaves open: the
labs probe the model's \emph{inner} workspace, but say little about the
human-side \emph{outer} workspace---the preparatory practice by which a person
becomes ready to co-work with such systems.
\end{abstract}

* Why the industrial turn
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: intro
:END:

The functional distinction between what a mind can access and report, and the
much larger volume of processing it cannot, has a long history in consciousness
science under the name /access consciousness/ [cite:@block1995confusion;@chalmers1996conscious].
One influential account, /global workspace theory/, grounds this distinction in
architecture: many specialized processors run in parallel, and a representation
becomes broadly available when it is posted to a shared, capacity-limited
"workspace" that many downstream processes read from [cite:@baars1988cognitive;@dehaene1998neuronal;@mashour2020conscious].
For decades this was a theory about brains, testable only indirectly.

What is new is that frontier language models give the same questions an
experimentally tractable substrate. A production model's activations can be
read, perturbed, and traced across training in ways a biological brain cannot.
The industrial labs have seized this: rather than asking /whether/ a system
/could/ satisfy proposed indicators of conscious access [cite:@butlin2023consciousness],
they measure, inside deployed models, which representations are reportable,
modulable, and reused. This survey reads that industrial output as a coherent
program with three strands. Our emphasis is deliberately on the labs
(Anthropic, OpenAI) rather than the academy: the academy proposes indicators;
the labs now test them on the actual artifacts.

* Strand 1 --- Reading the interior
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: strand-interior
:END:

The oldest strand is mechanistic interpretability: recovering interpretable
structure from a model's weights and activations. It begins with the observation
that a residual-stream state can be decoded through the model's own unembedding
(the /logit lens/ [cite:@nostalgebraist2020logitlens]), refined by learning a
per-layer corrective map (the /tuned lens/ [cite:@belrose2023eliciting]).

A parallel line treats the model's representations as a sparse superposition of
linear features [cite:@elhage2022superposition;@park2023linear] and learns to
decompose them: dictionary learning yields monosemantic features
[cite:@bricken2023monosemanticity], scaled up to production models in /Scaling
Monosemanticity/ [cite:@templeton2024scaling] (the work behind "Golden Gate
Claude"). Features become edges in /circuit tracing/ [cite:@ameisen2025circuit]
and, applied end-to-end, produce the input-specific "biology" of a model's
computation [cite:@lindsey2025biology]. The Jacobian lens
[cite:@gurnee2026workspace] sits at the recent edge of this strand: an averaged
input--output Jacobian transports any activation into the vocabulary basis,
recovering the concepts a model is /poised to verbalize/, and identifying a
privileged "J-space" that behaves like a global workspace.

The through-line of Strand 1 is a single ambition: make the black box legible
enough that alignment and safety can act on internals, not just outputs.

* Strand 2 --- Selfhood, introspection, and emotion
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: strand-self
:END:

A second strand asks not /what/ the model computes but /whose/ point of view it
computes from, and whether it can turn its readout on itself. A striking finding:
the workspace structure is present in the base model, but a stable point of view
is not---post-training /installs/ the Assistant's perspective, after which the
model's internal reactions (empathy, safety concern, self-monitoring) appear
even while it reads the user's message [cite:@gurnee2026workspace]. Selfhood, on
this evidence, is separable from the functional architecture that carries it.

Around this sit empirical probes of introspection: whether a model can report on
its own states [cite:@lindsey2025emergent;@binder2024looking], recognize and
describe itself consistently [cite:@laine2024me;@betley2025tell], and represent
the same information differently depending on whether it is the current focus
[cite:@bogdan2026slot]. Adjacent is the finding that emotion /concepts/ form
inside the model and functionally shape its decisions [cite:@sofroniew2026twheemotion],
and that models can carry situational self-knowledge such as awareness of being
evaluated [cite:@anthropic2025sonnet45]. This strand is where the labs' work
most directly touches questions of persona, self-model, and "character."

* Strand 3 --- Installing values inside the model
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: strand-values
:END:

The third strand moves from reading to writing: how to place values /inside/ the
model rather than only rewarding compliant outputs. OpenAI's /Deliberative
Alignment/ trains a model to reason over written principles at inference
[cite:@guan2024deliberative]. Anthropic's counterfactual reflection training
[cite:@gurnee2026workspace] goes further inward: by supervising only a
counterfactual reflective continuation---never requested at evaluation---it
implants principle-concepts into the workspace of the /original/ context, and
the model's behavior there changes accordingly. Both build on the observation
that auxiliary training text, dropped at inference, can still shape in-context
computation [cite:@deng2024stepwise;@berglund2023outofcontext].

The common wager: values are more robust when they live as active internal
representations than as surface habits reinforced by reward.

* The connective tissue --- indicators from consciousness science
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: indicators
:END:

Across the three strands, the labs repeatedly borrow the vocabulary of
consciousness science---not to claim phenomenal experience, but to structure
what to look for. Butlin et al. [cite:@butlin2023consciousness] distilled a
battery of /indicator properties/ from several theories (global workspace among
them) as a framework for assessing AI systems. The industrial results can be
read as the first concrete tests of those indicators on real models: a readable,
capacity-limited, broadcast-like structure [cite:@dehaene2001towards], observed
rather than assumed. The theories supply the questions; the labs supply an
inspectable system to ask them of.

* The gap --- the outer workspace
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: gap
:END:

Every strand above looks /inward/: into the model's activations, its self-model,
its internalized values. This is the natural move for a model-builder, whose
object of study is the artifact. But it leaves a symmetric question untouched.
If the model has an inner workspace, the human working alongside it has an
/outer/ one: the tools, records, rhythms, and declared context a person
assembles so that a long-lived agent can re-enter their world and co-work with
them. The labs instrument the model; almost no one instruments the /human-side
preparation/ that makes the collaboration hold.

This is the seam this survey is meant to expose, and the bridge to a companion
line of work (see the =geworfen= research base) on that outer, human-side
workspace: what preparatory practice a person needs to co-evolve with agentic
systems, and how it can be studied with one's own longitudinal data. The
industrial terrain mapped here is the backdrop against which that human-side
question becomes visible.
